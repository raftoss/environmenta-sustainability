Environmental initiatives
Environmental initiatives are key to protecting our planet

In today's world, environmental initiatives have become vital tools for safeguarding nature and combating ecological challenges. These projects address a wide range of issues, from reforestation to promoting renewable energy use.
Why Are Environmental Initiatives Important?
  1. Preserving Biodiversity: Restoring natural habitats helps protect rare species of plants and animals.
  2. Fighting Climate Change: Utilizing renewable energy sources like solar panels and wind turbines reduces carbon emissions.
  3. Raising Awareness: Participating in volunteer projects and donating to environmental causes draws attention to the importance of conservation efforts.
How Can We Help?
  • Joining Environmental Projects: Activities like planting trees, cleaning natural areas, or supporting renewable energy projects.
  • Supporting Sustainable Consumption: Choosing eco-friendly products and minimizing waste.
Together, we can make a tangible difference, leaving future generations a cleaner, safer planet.


Earth Hour
Description: An international initiative by the World Wildlife Fund (WWF) where people switch off lights for one hour to raise awareness about environmental issues. Millions participate across 190 countries.
Let's preserve nature today – for life tomorrow!
Successfull environmental projects
Clean Shores
Description: A Russian nationwide campaign focused on cleaning trash from shorelines. Volunteers clean up rivers and beaches and engage in eco-awareness efforts.

Eco-Dvor (Russia)
Description: A local project promoting waste separation and environmental education in Russian residential areas. Volunteers organize workshops and teach waste sorting.

Let’s Do It World
Description: A worldwide initiative focusing on litter cleanup across 150+ countries, uniting millions of volunteers.

The Ocean Cleanup
Description: A global project to remove plastic waste from oceans using innovative floating cleanup systems.
Environment in the Social Media Space
Caring for the environment starts with awareness on social media!
Kristy Drutman (@browngirl_green)

Kristy Drutman is an environmental activist and founder of the Brown Girl Green platform. She focuses on making the environmental movement more inclusive and educating people about sustainability, environmental justice, and the intersectionality between environmentalism and social justice. Kristy often shares personal stories, eco-friendly tips, and educational resources to inspire others to take action for a better planet.

Kiana Kazemi (@kiana.kaz)

Kiana Kazemi is an environmentalist and data scientist who co-founded both Beaker and Intersectional Environmentalist. Her Instagram focuses on sustainability, tech ethics, and climate solutions, often providing tips for addressing environmental challenges. Kiana's work is interactive, offering engaging content that invites followers to explore solutions through data and advocacy for climate justice​

Hannah Testa (@hannah4change)

Hannah Testa is an environmental advocate and the founder of Hannah4Change, a non-profit focused on tackling issues such as plastic pollution, deforestation, and climate change.
