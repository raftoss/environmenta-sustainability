"Silent Spring" by Rachel Carson
A landmark book in the environmental movement, it describes the negative effects of pesticides on nature, including animals and humans. Carson warns against the unchecked use of chemicals and advocates for more sustainable agricultural practices.
Reading for the planet — knowledge for the future!
Collection of books on the topic of the environment
Earth: The Sequel" by Fred Kaufman
This book explores the ecological transformation of the planet with a focus on new technological advancements that can help reduce environmental damage. The author discusses innovations like solar and wind power, biofuels, and new agricultural approaches.
"The Limits to Growth" by Donella Meadows, Dennis Meadows, and Jay Randers
A classic study on the possibilities and limitations of population growth, economic development, and natural resources on the planet. The authors predict the consequences of unlimited growth and offer strategies for sustainable development.
"Our Common Future" by the World Commission on Environment and Development
Known as the Brundtland Report, this document became the foundation for the concept of sustainable development. It analyzes issues related to poverty, inequality, and environmental destruction, offering solutions for achieving a sustainable future for all.
"Global Warming: The Myths and the Realities" by James Hansen
Written by one of the world’s leading climatologists, this book explains the scientific data on global warming, debunking myths and offering real solutions to the issue
"The Last Ice" — directed by Daniel Ferguson
A documentary about the threat of the Arctic's disappearance. The film shows how climate warming is affecting ice sheets, local ecosystems, and the lives of indigenous peoples who depend on ice.
Watching films for the planet — knowledge for the future!
Collection of films on the topic of the environment
Earth: The Sequel" by Fred Kaufman
This book explores the ecological transformation of the planet with a focus on new technological advancements that can help reduce environmental damage. The author discusses innovations like solar and wind power, biofuels, and new agricultural approaches.
"An Inconvenient Truth" — directed by Davis Guggenheim
This documentary, initiated by Al Gore, focuses on the problem of global warming. It explains the causes of climate change and its consequences for humanity while calling for action to reduce carbon emissions.
"Planet Earth" — directed by Jonathan Frakes
A documentary series about Earth's natural wealth. The series combines stunning footage of wildlife with pressing ecological issues, demonstrating the importance of preserving nature for future generations.
"The Green Planet" — directed by Michael J. Rice
A documentary exploring the role of plants in sustaining life on Earth. It reveals how vegetation impacts ecosystems and climate, emphasizing the importance of preserving flora for the planet’s ecology.
"The Daily" – The New York Times
Although not exclusively focused on the environment, this podcast often covers climate change, environmental policies, and significant ecological events. It offers insightful analysis on current news topics, including environmental issues affecting the world.

Listening to podcasts for the planet — knowledge for the future!
Collection of podcasts on the topic of the environment
"Green Dreamer" – Kamea Chayne
Hosted by sustainability advocate Kamea Chayne, this podcast focuses on environmental justice, regenerative living, and holistic sustainability. It covers diverse topics such as eco-conscious fashion, permaculture, and environmental activism, with inspiring conversations from thought leaders in the field.
"How to Save a Planet" – Elizabeth Yong and Meiya Maslin
A podcast that offers simple, practical solutions to combat climate change. In each episode, the hosts discuss what can be done on a personal level, as well as how to act at the societal and governmental levels to save the planet.
"Earth to Humans" – Inhabitat
This podcast explores the intersection of technology, sustainability, and design. It discusses innovative solutions to the world's environmental challenges, featuring interviews with experts working on eco-friendly products, architecture, and urban development.
"Climate Reality Project" – Al Gore
Hosted by former Vice President Al Gore, this podcast focuses on the global climate crisis. It features expert opinions, discussions on climate change solutions, and the latest advancements in sustainable practices. It’s an excellent resource for those wanting to understand the science behind climate change and how we can tackle it.
